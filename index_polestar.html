CFDI 4.0: más requisitos, más errores
CFDI 4.0: more requirements, more errors

Desde 2022, el CFDI 4.0 exige nuevos campos obligatorios como código postal del receptor, uso del CFDI y régimen fiscal correcto. Un error puede invalidar la factura y generar problemas ante el SAT. La automatización elimina este riesgo.

Since 2022, CFDI 4.0 requires new mandatory fields — postal code, CFDI use, fiscal regime. One error invalidates the invoice and creates SAT issues. Automation eliminates this risk entirely.

SAT · CFDI 4.0 · 2022

The day you spend your 183rd day in Mexico in a calendar year, your worldwide income becomes taxable here — including salary from your US employer. Most expats don't realize this until it's too late. We monitor it proactively.

Art. 9 CFF · LISR Título IV

RESICO: el régimen más sencillo del SAT
RESICO: Mexico's simplest tax regime

El Régimen Simplificado de Confianza (Art. 113-E LISR) ofrece tasas de ISR del 1 al 2.5% para personas físicas con ingresos hasta $3.5M al año. Pero no todos califican, y los errores cuestan. Nosotros verificamos tu elegibilidad y mantenemos tu cumplimiento activo.

RESICO (Art. 113-E LISR) offers ISR rates as low as 1–2.5% for individuals earning up to $3.5M MXN/year. Not everyone qualifies though, and errors are costly. We verify your eligibility and keep your compliance active.

RESICO · Art. 113-E LISR

Airbnb & Rentas Vacacionales / Vacation Rentals

Mexican law requires ISR on rental income, 16% IVA, and a state lodging tax (ISH) that varies by state. Airbnb retains some taxes but not all. If you own a vacation rental in Mexico — especially in a coastal zone — you likely have unmet obligations.

Earning salary or freelance income from a US or EU company while living in Mexico? If you're a tax resident, that income is fully taxable here. We structure your RFC registration, choose the right ISR regime, and keep you clean on both sides.

Your First Steps When Arriving in Mexico

A practical roadmap — in the right order — for foreigners new to Mexican fiscal obligations.

1
Get your RFC with SAT

Your Registro Federal de Contribuyentes is required to open a bank account, purchase property, or issue any invoice in Mexico. We handle the full registration process.

2
Verify your residency status

Count your days carefully. Once you hit 183 days in a calendar year, you become a Mexican tax resident and your worldwide income is taxable here.

3
Claim your treaty benefits

If you're from the US, Canada, Sweden, or Germany, a tax treaty likely reduces or eliminates Mexican withholding on certain income. Claim it proactively.

4
Choose the right tax regime

RESICO PF, Régimen General, or non-resident withholding (Título V)? The right choice depends on your income type, amount, and residency status. We run the numbers.

5
Set up your property correctly

Your domicilio fiscal (SAT registered address) must match the postal code on your CFDI invoices. Get this right before your first rental or sale transaction.

6
Coordinate with your home country

Don't close the loop on just the Mexican side. We help you communicate clearly with your US/Canada/EU tax advisor so nothing falls between the cracks.
