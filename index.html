Mexican Tax Services
Fully in English

Americans, Canadians, and Europeans living or investing in Mexico face a maze of local tax rules — on top of obligations back home. We specialize in exactly this overlap, guiding you through both sides in clear, plain English, wherever you are in Mexico.

🏠
Tax Residency & The 183-Day Rule
The moment you cross 183 days in Mexico in a calendar year, you become a Mexican tax resident — and your worldwide income becomes taxable here. We analyze your situation proactively so you're never caught off guard, and help you plan to optimize your residency status.
Art. 9 CFF · LISR Título IV vs. Título V · Casa habitación
🤝
US & Canada Tax Treaty Benefits
Mexico has tax treaties with the US, Canada, Sweden, Germany, and 60+ other countries. Don't let SAT withhold at the maximum rate when treaty rates are lower — sometimes zero. We file the right certificates and position your income correctly from day one.
Convenio MX-USA · MX-Canadá · Retención Art. 169 & 186 LISR
🏙
Buying Property in Mexico
Foreign nationals cannot hold title to property in restricted zones directly — a bank trust (fideicomiso) is required. We walk you through the RFC requirement, CFDI 4.0 compliance, notary withholding taxes, and everything you need before signing at the escritura.
Fideicomiso · CFDI 4.0 · CP domicilio fiscal · ISR enajenación
💻
Remote Work & Digital Nomads
Earning salary or freelance income from a US or EU company while living in Mexico? If you're a tax resident, that income is fully taxable here. We structure your RFC registration, choose the right ISR regime, and keep you clean on both sides of the border.
RFC · RESICO Art. 113-E · Régimen General · Declaración anual
🏠
Airbnb & Vacation Rental Income
Renting your Mexican property on Airbnb or VRBO? Mexican law requires ISR on rental income, 16% IVA, and a local lodging tax that varies by state. We calculate and file all correctly — and advise on the platform digital tax retentions too.
Arrendamiento Art. 114 LISR · ISH estatal · Plataformas digitales
🏭
FBAR & Foreign Account Reporting
Opening a Mexican bank account as a US citizen? If the balance exceeds $10,000 USD at any point, you must file FinCEN 114 (FBAR) back home — with penalties up to $10,000 per violation. We help you understand the obligation and coordinate with your US tax preparer.
FinCEN 114 · FATCA · FBAR · Cuenta bancaria MXN

Your First Steps When Arriving in Mexico

A practical roadmap — in the right order — for foreigners new to Mexican fiscal obligations.

1
Get your RFC with SAT Your Registro Federal de Contribuyentes is required to open a bank account, purchase property, or issue any invoice in Mexico. We handle the full registration process.
2
Verify your residency status Count your days carefully. Once you hit 183 days in a calendar year, you become a Mexican tax resident and your worldwide income is taxable here.
3
Claim your treaty benefits If you're from the US, Canada, Sweden, or Germany, a tax treaty likely reduces or eliminates Mexican withholding on certain income. Claim it proactively — not after SAT has already withheld.
4
Choose the right tax regime RESICO PF, Régimen General, or non-resident withholding (Título V)? The right choice depends on your income type, amount, and residency status. We run the numbers.
5
Set up your property correctly Your domicilio fiscal (registered tax address at SAT) must match the postal code on your CFDI invoices. Get this right before your first rental or sale transaction.
6
Coordinate with your home country Don't close the loop on just the Mexican side. We help you communicate clearly with your US/Canada/EU tax advisor so nothing falls between the cracks.
